Ablation Studies

Ablations isolate the contributions of both anchor/temporal design choices and the L/N compute trade-off, covering the streaming alignment artifacts that arise without anchor-audio zero padding or RoPE re-indexing as well as how window length (L) versus denoising depth (N) affect long-horizon stability under the same latency budget.

Qualitative Ablations (Long Rollouts)

We visualize the long-rollout failure modes discussed in Sec. 4.3. Removing either the style anchor or the temporal anchor increases drift and flicker, as shown in Tab. 4 and Fig. 6. Ablation of anchor-audio zero padding introduces misaligned speech features into the anchor pathway, which causes mouth jitter and local artifacts. In addition, removing RoPE re-indexing causes the anchor to become increasingly phase-misaligned as the window slides, which leads to gradual drift in appearance and color, as shown in Tab. 6 and Fig. 8. Finally, compared with one-step causal baselines, Self-Forcing (1-step) exhibits noticeable color drift, and Causal ODE tends to produce blurred frames, whereas AvatarForcing better preserves appearance consistency and motion sharpness.

L/N Decoupling Sweep

The sweep in Fig. 4 / Tab. 3 fixes the dual-anchor design and varies window length L and denoising steps N to track how latency scales with stability: larger L tends to improve long-horizon consistency more than simply stacking denoising passes, but excessively large L can over-smooth motion, which the merged comparison below captures.
